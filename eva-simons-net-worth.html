Eva Simons Finance Overview

Early Life and Background

Eva Simons was born in Amsterdam in April 1984. Raised in a musical family, her mother, Ingrid Simons, was a Dutch backing singer, while her grandfather, Johnny Meijer, was a well-known accordionist.

Her early exposure to music set the stage for her future career. She graduated from the prestigious Conservatorium van Amsterdam, further honing her musical talents.

Breakthrough and Major Achievements

Eva’s career breakthrough came with the song Silly Boy, which gained significant attention on YouTube in 2009. The demo, initially mistaken for a track by Lady Gaga or Rihanna, helped her secure a contract with EMI.

Her major collaboration with Afrojack on the hit single Take Over Control catapulted her to international fame. This track topped dance charts worldwide and earned platinum status in Australia and gold in the US.

In 2012, her feature on will.i.am’s This Is Love became a chart-topping hit in several countries, including the UK, Belgium, and Ireland.

This collaboration significantly boosted her profile and opened doors to more high-profile projects.

Notable Songs and Albums

Throughout her career, Eva Simons has released several popular singles. Some of her most notable tracks include:

  • Policeman: A dancehall hit featuring Sidney Samson.
  • Bludfire: Another successful collaboration with Sidney Samson.
  • I Don’t Like You: Produced by Zedd and released under Interscope Records.
