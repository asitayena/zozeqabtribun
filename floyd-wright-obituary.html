Floyd Wright Obituary: Host Remembered for His Contributions

Posted by Kelle Repass on Tuesday, August 27, 2024

Floyd Wright obituary shared the details of his sudden demise and how it affected the people around him. 

Connecticut’s broadcasting industry has lost one of its most beloved and iconic voices, Floyd Wright, famously known as “Friendly Floyd,” who passed away at 72. 

His passing has left a void that will be difficult to fill, as he touched the hearts of many listeners during his long and illustrious career.

The sudden death of Floyd Wright has left the Hartford community in shock. Many remember him as a legendary radio host who significantly impacted the industry and the people he met.

An individual recalled how Floyd’s live broadcast at the Frank Marrata’s Auto Show at the Civic Center was the moment that sparked their interest in radio. 

They were in awe watching him work through the mobile studio’s windows and were amazed when he asked them to choose the next album to play. 

It was a moment that stayed with them and inspired their future endeavors in the radio industry.

After the company that employed Floyd was sold, a party was held, and all the employees were invited. 

The individual expressed that it was one of the happiest nights of their life, and they felt honored to have worked alongside Floyd.

Floyd resided in Newington, and the individual would occasionally run into him at the supermarket. 

They recalled how he would always share his latest experiences at WJMJ, where he worked until his passing. 

Floyd’s passion for radio and his friendly demeanor was evident in all his interactions, impacting those he met.

His on-air career spanned several decades, and he had a knack for winning over his audience with his friendly demeanor and warm personality. 

He was a fixture on Top 40 WKCI (KC101)/New Haven, Country WYNY/New York, and Country WWYZ (Country 92-5)/Hartford, where he spent over 20 years in the coveted afternoon drive time slot. 

His charisma and charm also landed him gigs at WDRC-F/Hartford and WJMJ-F, which is owned by the Archdiocese of Hartford, where he worked until his passing in 2023.

Friendly Floyd’s impact on Connecticut’s broadcasting industry cannot be understated. He was a true pioneer, and his influence was felt across the state and beyond. 

He had a unique ability to connect with his listeners on a personal level, and his infectious laugh and genuine kindness endeared him to many.

Floyd’s legacy will live on through the countless lives he touched during his career. His listeners looked forward to his daily broadcasts, and many have fond memories of calling in to chat with him on the air. 

He was a beloved figure in the community and will be deeply missed.

Floyd Wright’s Obituary mentioned that he leaves behind his loving wife, Susan, along with several brothers, nieces, and nephews. 

His family, friends, and fans are mourning his loss, but they will always cherish the memories he created and the impact he had on their lives.
