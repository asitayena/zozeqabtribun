Early Life & Biography

Nathan Lane was born in Jersey City, New Jersey. His birth name is Joseph Lane. He was born to his father, Daniel, and his mother, Nora Lane. His father has been a truck driver who died in 1967 due to alcoholism. At that time, Lane was only eleven years old. His mother, named Nora, has served as a secretary. Due to bipolar disorder, she died in 2000. Lane also has two elder brothers, Robert, and Daniel Jr. He went to Catholic schools in New Jersey City. While he was studying, he earned the title of “Best Actor” in 1974, and received the “Prep Hall of Fame Professional Achievement Award” in 2011.

Career

In the 1990s, he got to play in a series of successful Broadway shows. Lane appeared “On Borrowed Time,” for which he received his first-ever Tony Awards nomination Drama Desk and Outer Critics Circle Awards.

In 1992, he was the winner of the Obie Award for his Sustained Excellence of Performance.

In 1999, he voiced the character of Snowbell in film Stuart Little.

In 2001, he starred in “The Producers” that earned him another Tony Award, Drama Desk, and Outer Critics Circle Awards.

For his performance in this musical film version, he earned his second Golden Globe nomination under the category of “Best Performance by an Actor in a Motion Picture, Comedy, or Musical.”

In 2010, he was starred in “The Addams Family” and received a Drama League Award for Distinguished Achievement in Musical Theatre.

He was starred in guest roles on Mad About You, Frasier, The Good Wife, Curb Your Enthusiasm, Absolutely Fabulous, Sex and the City, Difficult People, 30 Rock, and Modern Family for which he received Emmy Award nominations for six times.

He has lent his voice in “George and Martha” on HBO and two Disney series, Timon & Pumbaa, and Teacher’s Pet. He also received Daytime Emmy Awards for Timon and Pumbaa, George and Martha, and Teacher’s Pet.
