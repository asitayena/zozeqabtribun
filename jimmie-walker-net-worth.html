FAQs about Jimmie Walker

When and where was he born?

He was born on June 25, 1947, in Brooklyn, New York, United States.

What is Jimmie Walker best known for?

Walker is best recognized for his portrayal of J.J. Evans on Good Times, where his character was known for the catchphrase Dyn-o-mite!

Has Jimmie Walker received any awards or nominations for his work?

Yes, Walker was nominated for Golden Globe Awards in 1975 and 1976 for his role in Good Times.

What other TV shows and movies has Jimmie Walker appeared in?

After Good Times, Walker starred in shows like At Ease and Bustin’ Loose and appeared in films such as Let’s Do It Again, Airplane!, and Super Shark, among others.

Did he pursue any other careers apart from acting?

Yes, before his acting career took off, Walker worked in radio, including roles as a radio engineer and host on stations like WHIO, WOAI, WLS, and KKAR.

What is his educational background?

Walker dropped out of high school but later attended night classes at Theodore Roosevelt High School. He also enrolled in a government-run program for minorities to study radio engineering and announcing.

What is Walker’s stance on marriage and children?

As of 2012, Walker has stated that he has never been married nor fathered any children, although he has had many girlfriends.

What are Walker’s political views?

He describes himself as a realist independent and logicist, holding conservative views on many issues. He has expressed opposition to Barack Obama’s presidency and support for Donald Trump.

Has he written any books?

Yes, Walker published his autobiography, Dyn-o-mite! Good Times, Bad Times, Our Times – A Memoir, in 2012.

What is Jimmie Walker’s height?

He is 6 ft 1 in (185 cm) tall.
