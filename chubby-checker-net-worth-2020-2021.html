Career

In 1959, after signing with Cameo-Parkway Records, Chubby Cheker recorded his two singles as “Dancing Dinosaur” and “The Class.” After getting inspired by Cameo, he released his version of “The Twist,” which got immense success on the US Billboard chart in September 1960. It was ranked on the number one position on the chart. This song was originally written as well as performed by Hank Ballard.

The reason behind the huge popularity of his version was his dance movies that added new life to the song. Checker was dubbed as “The King of the Twist.” In January 1962, the song again topped the chart. It was considered a remarkable achievement.

Following it, Checker recorded several other successful songs as “Let’s Twist Again,” “Twist It Up,” and “Twistin’ U.S.A.” in the early 1960s. Despite their popularity, none of these songs matched the success received by the song “The Twist.”

In 1990, a prominent advertiser ‘Nabisco’ featured Chubby Checker to twist the Oreo cookie. This ad became one of the most successful promotions of the company till date.
